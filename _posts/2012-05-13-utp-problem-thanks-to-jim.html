You should think about wait and wait' in connection with ok and ok'.

Here is a suitable interpretation of their combinations:

ok /\ wait: the process is in a stable intermediate state of its
sequential predecessor.

ok /\ not wait: the process has been properly started from a stable final
state of its sequential predecessor.

not ok: the process is in an unstable state of its sequential predecessor.

ok' /\ wait': the process is in a stable intermediate state.

ok' /\ not wait': the process is in a stable final state.

not ok': the process is in an unstable state.

As you can see, although wait and ok are boolean variables, there are
only three interesting combinations of their values (and the same for
wait' and ok').
